Sharify: Simplify Sharing

For Sharify we wanted a familiar look and feel. The user experience shouldn't be that different from other streaming apps like Apple Music or Spotify. We analyzed these apps and came up with our own design guidelines how we wanted to app to look like.

It was very easy to usertest this app because most people are familiar with music-streaming services and as far as interaction went, they did not need a lot of assistance. People were enthusiastic about the idea of the app and told us it was a very useful tool for music lovers.

At the end of the semester we wanted a high fidelity prototype to show our teachers and the client. During this process, we were also developing an actual app that used Spotify's API to match text to albums and tracks. This way we could share a link via Apple Music, get the song values and "translate" it into Spotify.

To pitch our concept to our classmates, the teachers and the client, I created a video showing the members of our group explaining what the problem is we want to tackle and in what way we wanted to do that.

The video is available on this page, sadly, we only made a Dutch variant.

Quantify: Track your studying

The Quantified Student concept was very interesting to me. How can we measure how students get distracted and what exactly distracts them. To build something meaningful for this assignment me and my groupmates decided to take a look in the mirror and ask what would distract us. With years of experience under our belts, we were confident we would create something great!

The idea of an app that tracks your study sessions was very compelling to us. We all would like this kind of app to track our own studying sessions and we noticed that tracking data was popular amongst the smartphone community. You can track your steps, heart rate and sport sessions. From the time you use your phone in a day to how many minutes of YouTube videos you watched is all trackable. Why wouldn't your study session be trackable?

At the end of the semester we wanted a high fidelity prototype build that would feature our desired tracking data. This data would be stuff like the noise levels in your session, times you picked up your phone and notifications you would get. While we were building this prototype we were also developing a real app that would track your surrounding noise in a session.

At the end of the semester was the Quantified Student Day. The video is attached to this page. It was a great success and even made it to various Quantified Student media outlets.

Schmeck: Motivated Working

Futuris helps people who need care- and or work guidance. They offer housing and workplaces for people with a developmental disorder. The only problem is that some of their clients do not show up for their work because they are not motivated enough.

Our solution was to create an app that lets clients collect digital currency, or Schmeckle's, and let them trade those in for small extra's at work. Clients gather Schmeckle's for the time they are working. If a client works for an amount of days in a row, they will gain streaks an other bonuses.

There is a lot more about this project, so click on it to learn more!

Schmeck: Motivated Working

The task that Futuris gave us was simple: make a fun tool that inspires our clients to go to work. Most of the clients have autism or a mental handicap and because Futuris provides them with housing they need to have a job. Futuris provides those jobs, but the clients aren't familiar with the concept of working and showing up on time for that. Futuris tries to help them with that, but also has their hands full of other matters in the housing itself.

Through interviews we found out that a lot of the clients would play video games in their spare time. Most of them would do this until late in the night and because of this would show up late or wouldn't show up at all at their workplaces. It seemed like a good idea to motivate them to go to work with some sort of game, they enjoyed them in their free time, so maybe they'll get hooked on a mobile motivation game!

We build this mobile as a high fidelity prototype and showed this to Futuris. They were enthusiastic and wanted to see if we could realize this in code. Because at this point there wasn't that much time left we decided to build the app in React. We converted our designs into this new space and around that we build a presentation to show our exact concept and designs/prototype. The video attached is that final presentation.

BW-Ventures: Dashboard

For this assignment, it was clear we needed to make a dashboard in a desktop website. Because filling in the different forms was an important and time consuming task, we felt that this would be done on a desktop rather than on a phone or tablet. The essential idea of the assignment was to translate the Excel sheets to a fully functioning web version.

We started by setting up a database system in PHP and worked from there to create a fully functioning back-end, this was the part I mostly partook in. I also helped out creating some parts of the front-end.

It was very interesting testing this tool with the start-ups that BW-Ventures were actually helping, they gave very useful insights. With the help of these user tests we were able to create a tool that would help these start-ups the best way possible.

Stallcatchers: Fight Alzheimers

Sadly the Stallcatchers website has been renewed, but luckily I still have the local version of the landingspage. You can view it here.

Participating in the Stallcatchers project was part of my internship at Game Solutions Lab. Game Solutions Lab is activily trying to help better the world by building games and apps that help people in original and fun ways.

You can find their website here.

When you play Stallcatchers you are trying to see if the veins of the brains of mice aren't glugged. When you see one that doesn't flow all the way you hit the spot and the time when it happened. When a lot of users notice the same stall, the video is sent to Alzheimer researches to add this to their research. Want to try it? Sign up on this website.

The t-shirt I made is attached to this page. The various names of the supporters of Stallcatchers are listed on the back of the shirt. The way they are designed is to make them appear as the stalls they are trying to catch.
